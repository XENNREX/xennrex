1 Introduction

At Xennrex (Pty) Ltd ("Xennrex", "we", "us", or "our"), we respect your privacy and are committed to protecting your Personal Information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, store, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website at xennrex.org and use our private cloud storage and collaboration services (collectively, the "Service").

This Privacy Policy is drafted in compliance with the Protection of Personal Information Act 4 of 2013 ("POPI Act" or "POPIA"), as well as the Consumer Protection Act 68 of 2008 and the Electronic Communications and Transactions Act 25 of 2002.

Our Commitment

We do not sell your Personal Information. We do not use your data for advertising. We do not scan your files for commercial purposes. Your data sovereignty is our highest priority.

By accessing or using our Service, you acknowledge that you have read and understood this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with any aspect of this Privacy Policy, you must discontinue use of the Service immediately.

2 Information We Collect

We collect and process Personal Information that is necessary for the legitimate operation of our Service and to fulfil our contractual obligations to you. The types of information we collect are categorised as follows:

Account Information

Your full name, email address, telephone number, company or organisation name, and physical address. This information is provided directly by you during Account registration and profile management.

Payment Information

Payment processing is handled entirely by PayFast (Pty) Ltd. We do not store your credit card numbers, CVV codes, or banking details. We retain only transaction references and payment confirmation records for accounting purposes.

Usage Data

Storage quota utilisation, login timestamps, session duration, and feature usage patterns. This data is collected solely for the purpose of Service operation, performance optimisation, and billing verification.

Technical Data

Internet Protocol (IP) address, browser type and version, operating system, device type, and time zone setting. This data is used exclusively for security monitoring, fraud prevention, and troubleshooting.

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

When you register for an Account, subscribe to a plan, contact our support team, or interact with the Service in any way, you may provide us with:

  • Identity Information: Name, surname, company name, job title;
  • Contact Information: Email address, telephone number, physical address;
  • Account Credentials: Username and password (stored using bcrypt hashing);
  • Communication Records: Any information you provide when contacting our support or sales teams;
  • Preferences: Notification preferences, language settings, and display preferences.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you access or use the Service, certain information is collected automatically through cookies, server logs, and similar technologies:

  • Log Data: IP address, browser type, referring/exit pages, operating system, date/time stamps;
  • Device Information: Hardware model, operating system version, unique device identifiers;
  • Usage Patterns: Pages visited, features used, clicks, and navigation paths within the Service;
  • Location Data: Approximate geographic location derived from your IP address for security and fraud prevention purposes only.

2.3 Client Data

All files, documents, images, videos, and other content that you or your Authorised Users upload, store, or share through the Service ("Client Data") remains your exclusive property. We do not access, scan, analyse, or process Client Data for any purpose other than storing it and making it available to you through the Service, unless required by law or with your explicit consent.

4 Legal Basis for Processing

Under Section 11 of the POPI Act, we process Personal Information only where a lawful ground for processing exists. Our legal bases for processing your Personal Information are:

4.1 Consent (Section 11(1)(a))

Where you have provided your explicit consent, we process your Personal Information for the purposes specified at the time consent was obtained. For example, consent to receive marketing communications. You have the right to withdraw consent at any time by contacting us at info@xennrex.org.

4.2 Contractual Necessity (Section 11(1)(b))

Processing is necessary for the performance of our contract with you — that is, to provide the Service you have subscribed to. Without this processing, we cannot create your Account, store your data, or provide the core functionality of the Service.

4.3 Legal Obligation (Section 11(1)(c))

Processing is necessary to comply with a legal obligation to which we are subject. This includes tax and accounting record-keeping obligations, responding to lawful court orders or subpoenas, and cooperating with law enforcement agencies.

4.4 Legitimate Interest (Section 11(1)(f))

Processing is necessary for the purposes of our legitimate interests or those of a third party, except where such interests are overridden by your interests, rights, and freedoms. Our legitimate interests include:

  • Ensuring the security and integrity of our Service and infrastructure;
  • Preventing fraud and abuse;
  • Improving Service performance and user experience through anonymised analytics;
  • Exercising our legal rights and defending against legal claims.

6 Data Retention

6.1 We retain your Personal Information only for as long as is necessary to fulfil the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements.

6.2 Active Account Retention

While your Account is active, we retain all Personal Information necessary to provide the Service and maintain your Account.

6.3 Post-Closure Retention

Following closure of your Account:

  • Client Data: Permanently deleted within 30 (thirty) days of Account closure, unless you request earlier deletion or we are legally required to retain it for a longer period;
  • Account Information: Retained for 30 (thirty) days after Account closure, after which it is permanently deleted;
  • Transaction Records: Retained for 5 (five) years from the date of transaction in compliance with tax legislation;
  • Server Logs: Anonymised and/or deleted within 90 (ninety) days;
  • Backup Copies: Automatically purged from our redundant backup systems within 90 (ninety) days of Account deletion.

6.4 Legal Retention

In certain circumstances, we may retain your Personal Information for a longer period where we have a legal obligation to do so (for example, tax records) or where retention is necessary for the establishment, exercise, or defence of legal claims. When we no longer have a legitimate basis for retaining your Personal Information, it will be securely deleted or anonymised.

7 Data Security

7.1 We take the security of your Personal Information seriously and implement appropriate technical and organisational measures to protect it against unauthorised or unlawful processing, accidental loss, destruction, damage, alteration, or disclosure. These measures include:

  • AES-256 Encryption: All data is encrypted at rest using the Advanced Encryption Standard (AES) with a 256-bit key;
  • TLS 1.3: All data transmitted between your devices and our servers is protected using Transport Layer Security;
  • Encrypted Backups: All backup copies of your data are encrypted using the same AES-256 standard;
  • Access Controls: Strict role-based access controls limit access to Personal Information to authorised personnel only on a need-to-know basis;
  • Two-Factor Authentication (2FA): Available for all Accounts to provide an additional layer of access protection;
  • Regular Security Audits: We conduct periodic vulnerability assessments, penetration testing, and security reviews of our systems;
  • Secure Infrastructure: Our enterprise cloud infrastructure is hosted in physically secure facilities with controlled access, redundant power, and environmental controls;
  • Staff Training: All employees with access to Personal Information undergo regular data protection training.

7.2 In the event of a Personal Information breach, we have in place procedures to respond promptly. We will notify affected individuals and the Information Regulator in accordance with our obligations under Section 22 of the POPI Act.

7.3 Despite our security measures, no system is completely impenetrable. We cannot guarantee the absolute security of your data transmitted over the internet, and any transmission is at your own risk.

8 Data Location & Cross-Border Transfers

Data Sovereignty Guarantee

All Client Data and Personal Information is stored exclusively within the Republic of South Africa. Our primary infrastructure and all redundant backup systems are located within South African jurisdiction. No cross-border transfer of your Personal Information occurs unless expressly authorised by you in writing or as required by law.

8.1 Xennrex's secure distributed cloud infrastructure with redundant backup systems is located entirely within South Africa. Your data never leaves South African jurisdiction unless you explicitly choose to access it from outside the country.

8.2 We do not transfer your Personal Information to any country outside of South Africa. In the unlikely event that a cross-border transfer becomes necessary (for example, to comply with a lawful international legal request), we will ensure that:

  • The recipient country has adequate data protection laws substantially similar to the POPI Act; or
  • Appropriate safeguards are in place, such as binding corporate rules or standard contractual clauses approved by the Information Regulator; or
  • Your explicit consent has been obtained prior to the transfer.

8.3 Where you access the Service from outside South Africa, your Personal Information will be transferred to and processed in South Africa. By using the Service, you consent to this transfer and processing in South Africa.

9 Your Rights Under the POPI Act

The POPI Act grants you the following rights in relation to your Personal Information. You may exercise any of these rights by contacting our Information Officer at info@xennrex.org.

Right of Access

You have the right to request access to the Personal Information we hold about you and to receive a copy of that information.

Right to Correction

You have the right to request that we correct any inaccurate, misleading, or outdated Personal Information.

Right to Deletion

You have the right to request the deletion of your Personal Information where we no longer have a lawful basis to retain it.

Right to Object

You have the right to object to the processing of your Personal Information on reasonable grounds.

Right to Complain

You have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator if you believe we have violated the POPI Act.

Right to Withdraw Consent

Where processing is based on consent, you have the right to withdraw that consent at any time.

9.1 How to Exercise Your Rights

To exercise any of your rights, please send a written request to our Information Officer at info@xennrex.org with the subject line "POPI Act Data Subject Request". Your request must:

  • Be made in writing (email is acceptable);
  • Provide sufficient information to verify your identity (we may request a copy of your identification document);
  • Describe the right you wish to exercise with sufficient detail;
  • Specify the Personal Information to which the request relates.

We will respond to your request within a reasonable time and in any event within the timeframes prescribed by the POPI Act. We may charge a reasonable fee for manifestly unfounded or excessive requests.

9.2 Lodging a Complaint with the Information Regulator

If you are not satisfied with our response, or if you believe we have processed your Personal Information in violation of the POPI Act, you have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Regulator:

10 Cookies & Similar Technologies

10.1 We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to enhance your experience with the Service, improve security, and collect analytical data. Cookies are small text files placed on your device that store certain information about your visit.

10.2 Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: These cookies are strictly necessary for the operation of the Service. They enable core functionality such as session management, security authentication, and CSRF protection. You cannot opt out of essential cookies.
  • Preference Cookies: These cookies remember your settings and preferences (such as language and display theme) to provide a more personalised experience.
  • Analytics Cookies: These cookies collect anonymous, aggregated information about how visitors use our website and Service. We use this data to improve Service performance and user experience. Analytics cookies are only placed with your explicit consent.
  • Security Cookies: We use Google reCAPTCHA cookies to protect our forms from spam and abuse. These cookies are necessary for fraud prevention.

10.3 For detailed information about the cookies we use, including names, purposes, and durations, please see our Cookie Policy.

10.4 You can manage your cookie preferences through the cookie consent banner displayed when you first visit our website, or by adjusting your browser settings. Please note that disabling essential cookies will prevent you from using the Service.
