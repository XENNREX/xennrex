1 What Are Cookies

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer, smartphone, tablet, or other device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently and to provide information to the website owners.

Cookies serve many purposes. They can help a website remember your preferences, keep you logged in, improve security, understand how you interact with the site, and generally enhance your browsing experience. Some cookies are essential for a website to function properly, while others are optional and used for analytics, preferences, or security enhancements.

In addition to cookies, we may use other similar technologies such as local storage, session storage, and web beacons (also known as pixel tags or clear GIFs) to collect and store information about your use of the Service.

2 Types of Cookies We Use

We use different categories of cookies on our website and Service. Each category serves a specific purpose. Below is an overview of the types of cookies we use:

Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the Service to function. They enable core features such as session management, CSRF protection, and security authentication. Without these cookies, the Service cannot operate.

CANNOT BE DISABLED

Preference Cookies

These cookies remember your settings and preferences — such as language selection, theme settings, and display preferences — so you don't have to reconfigure them on every visit.

OPTIONAL

Analytics Cookies

These cookies collect anonymous, aggregated information about how visitors use our website. They help us understand which pages are most popular, identify technical issues, and improve the overall user experience.

OPTIONAL

Security Cookies

These cookies are used by security services — specifically Google reCAPTCHA — to protect our forms from spam, abuse, and automated attacks. They help distinguish between humans and bots.

REQUIRED FOR FORMS

2.1 Essential Cookies

Essential cookies are fundamental to the operation of the Service. They enable:

  • User authentication and secure session management;
  • CSRF (Cross-Site Request Forgery) protection for form submissions;
  • Maintenance of your login state across page navigations;
  • Security measures to detect and prevent unauthorised access;
  • Basic Service functionality that enables cloud storage and file management.

These cookies are first-party cookies set by Xennrex Cloud and do not track you across the internet. Because they are essential for the Service to function, they cannot be disabled through our cookie preferences. You may configure your browser to block these cookies, but doing so will prevent you from accessing and using the Service.

2.2 Preference Cookies

Preference cookies enable the Service to remember choices you make — such as your preferred language, display theme (light/dark), and other customisable settings — to provide enhanced, personalised features. These cookies are optional and can be disabled, though doing so will mean your preferences are not remembered between visits.

2.3 Analytics Cookies

Analytics cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our website by collecting and reporting information anonymously. These cookies:

  • Track which pages are visited most frequently;
  • Identify technical errors or broken functionality;
  • Measure page load times and performance metrics;
  • Analyse traffic sources and user flow through the site.

These cookies collect information in an aggregated, anonymised form. They do not identify you personally. Analytics cookies are placed only with your explicit consent, which you can provide or withdraw at any time through the cookie consent banner.

2.4 Security Cookies

We use Google reCAPTCHA on certain forms (such as our registration and contact forms) to protect against spam, automated abuse, and fraudulent submissions. reCAPTCHA sets cookies to determine whether the user is a human or an automated system. These cookies are necessary for the security of our Service and the protection of all users.

4 Third-Party Cookies

In addition to our own first-party cookies, we use services from the following third parties that may set cookies on your device:

4.1 Google reCAPTCHA

We use Google reCAPTCHA v3 on our registration, login, and contact forms to protect against automated spam and abuse. reCAPTCHA sets security cookies (listed in the table above) to analyse traffic patterns and determine whether the user is a human or a bot.

reCAPTCHA collects the following information:

  • Hardware and software information about your device;
  • Information about your browser and the reCAPTCHA widgets installed on pages you visit;
  • Information about your interaction with the reCAPTCHA challenge (where applicable).

Google uses this information solely to provide the reCAPTCHA service and for general security purposes. For more information about Google's data practices, please see the Google Privacy Policy and the Google Terms of Service.

4.2 Google Analytics

With your consent, we use Google Analytics to collect anonymous statistical data about how visitors use our website. Google Analytics sets cookies (listed in the table above) to track user interactions. The data collected is aggregated and does not personally identify you. We use Google Analytics with IP anonymisation enabled.

Important Note

We do not use any third-party advertising cookies. We do not share cookie data with advertisers, data brokers, or marketing networks. We do not use cookies to build profiles for advertising purposes.

5 How to Manage Cookies

You have the right to decide whether to accept or reject cookies. You can exercise your cookie preferences in the following ways:

5.1 Cookie Consent Banner

When you first visit our website, you will see a cookie consent banner that allows you to:

  • Accept All Cookies: Consent to the use of all cookie categories;
  • Customise Preferences: Choose which optional cookie categories you wish to enable or disable;
  • Accept Essential Only: Use only the essential cookies required for the Service to function.

5.2 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • Delete all cookies stored on your device;
  • Block all cookies from being set;
  • Block third-party cookies while allowing first-party cookies;
  • Receive a notification before a cookie is set.

Please refer to your browser's help documentation for specific instructions:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.3 Important Limitations

Essential Cookies Required

Please note that if you disable essential cookies through your browser settings, you will be unable to access or use the Xennrex Cloud Service. Essential cookies are strictly necessary for authentication, session management, and security. Disabling these cookies will prevent you from logging in, accessing your files, or using any Service features.

6 Consent

6.1 By using our Service, you consent to the placement of essential cookies on your device. Essential cookies are necessary for the Service to function and cannot be disabled.

6.2 Analytics cookies are placed on your device only with your explicit opt-in consent. The first time you visit our website, you will be presented with a cookie consent banner that allows you to choose whether to enable or disable analytics cookies. You may change your preference at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" button below.

6.3 Your consent preferences are stored in the xennrex_consent cookie and are valid for 1 (one) year, after which you will be prompted to renew your preferences. You may withdraw or modify your consent at any time.

6.4 If you do not provide consent for optional cookies, your experience of the Service will not be negatively affected. The core functionality of the Service remains fully available regardless of your analytics cookie preference.
